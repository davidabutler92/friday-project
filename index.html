Magic: The Gathering Beginners guide on how to play

Object of the game

Magic is a turn based game with many different styles of playing. You can play on teams or have an all out free-for-all. The object is to defeat your opponent. Eveyone starts with 20 life and 0 life means defeat. To do this you will be using creature cards and different kinds of spells.

Card types and use

Land cards are a resource in magic. At the start of each turn you can play 1 land card. Depending on the mana cost (mana gained from land cards) you can summon different creatures and spells. Stronger creature and spells cards cost more mana and in turn will take more land cards. To use a land card, once you have it on the playing field, “tap” it from its upright position and turn it horizontal. A creature card that cost 1 swamp land means you have to tap 1 swamp land to play that creature. Once a land card is used in that turn it stays tapped until your next turn, in which you will untap all of your land cards and use them for spells/creatures.

Creatures come in all sorts of forms. In the bottom right of the card you will see numbers like 2/1 or stronger creatures may look like 7/7. In the example “2/1” the 2 stands for power and the 1 stands for toughness. For example a creature with a 2 power will do 2 damage and in turn will take 2 life from the opponent if thier life is attacked directly. The toughness is like the creatures defense. For example an opponent can defend against your attack with his/her creature if the toughness is equal to or greater than the attacker (2/1 attacking a /2>). To attack with a creature you have to tap lands equal to cost of spell is in top right.

Spells have many different applications and can really turn the tide is a losing battle. There are some spells that will boost either the power, toughness or both of that target creature. For example if you use a spell card that says “target creature gets "+2/+2” and the creature has a power/toughness of 1/1, that creature will now be a 3/3. Sorceries are a spell that can directly hit an opponents life and they can’t defend against it unless they have a counter spell card. Just like creatures you have to tap land cards equivalent to the mana cost of the spell card (mana cost is also in the top right corner, represented by land symbols and the corresponding color).
